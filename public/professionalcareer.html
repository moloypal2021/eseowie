Professional Career

A consistent advocate of the convergence of theory and practice in Governance especially in emerging economies, Dr Owie served between 2011 and 2016, as Executive Director & Chief Responsibility Officer of the Centre for International Trade & Policy (CITaP), Geneva, Switzerland. CiTAP, is a Think Tank and Research Centre that provides cutting-edge, actionable advice to Governments, Non-State Actors and Civil Society Organizations on, amongst other things, the mainstreaming of Environmental Social and Governance (ESG) considerations in Trade Policy Formulation and Sequencing, Sustainability and Developmental Strategy.

In the wake of the 2008 global financial crisis, Dr Owie co-founded Climate Change Africa (CCA), a Third Sector Policy Action and Research Centre that is committed to taking urgent action to cut greenhouse gas emissions, speed the global shift to renewables and a sustainable green economy. CCA has, over the years, demonstrated commitment to building a more just and equitable world, one where all voices, especially those of the Global South, are heard and respected in the global climate change debate. CCA recognizes that a diverse and inclusive movement is critical to solving the climate crisis, and that to build this coalition, Nations must ensure that those directly impacted – particularly those who have been excluded in the past – are at the center of the movement for change., CCA is thus at the forefront of raising awareness that indigenous peoples, low-income families and other historically marginalized groups in Africa represent frontline communities who experience harmful climate impacts first and are worse hit. The international community must consequently commit to understanding and undoing the historic injustices which contribute to this reality and, in proffering solutions, address systemic inequities.

A leading international trade expert

Dr. Owie is a leading international trade expert, providing technical capacity in trade policy formulation in the Global South, especially in Africa. In 2016, he co-founded The Cavendish Institute, a highly specialized provider of technical assistance and negotiating advice on international trade issues. Cavendish provides timely, pragmatic and cost-efficient advice, representation and capacity building in the context of trade negotiations, the formulation and implementation of national trade policy and the settlement of disputes. It has risen to become a high caliber, reputable institute, comprised of a core team of experienced trade practitioners based in Geneva, London, Washington DC, Paris, Toronto, Johannesburg, Nairobi and Abuja, with academic and consulting capacity drawn from its global network of trade experts. The Institute serves Governments of developed and developing countries, International Governmental Organizations as well as Third Sector Groups, Trade Association and Businesses.

Garnered advanced competencies in all areas of international trade law, policy and economics, within and beyond the World Trade Organization (WTO) context.

In his capacity as President of the Cavendish Institute, Dr Owie has garnered advanced competencies in all areas of international trade law, policy and economics, within and beyond the World Trade Organization (WTO) context. He is proficient in matters ranging from the Doha Development Agenda to WTO dispute settlement, from GATT to GATS, trade remedies, trade competition, agriculture, market access, domestic regulation, trade and environment to trade and investment. He is also a specialialist in numerous trade-related issues including services regulation and related policy-making, the design of goods offers and their negotiation, trade and climate change, SPS and NTBs, trade facilitation, subsidies, and government procurement. He has demonstrated expertise in WTO accessions, from observership via bilaterals to pre- and post- accession implementation. A respected practitioner in the area of bilateral and regional trade systems, He has advised on African Union trade law and policy, including the process leading to the negotiation, ratification and entry into force of theAfrican Continental Free Trade Agreement (AfCFTA). Through his academic and public engagements, he has exhibited profound understanding and expertise in various aspects of the EU’s trade relations with the African, as well the Caribbean and Pacific Group. His expertise is further demonstrated in his work on regional integration processes and agreement such as SADC, COMESA, Pacific Islands Forum, ASEAN and CARICOM.

Beyond his interest in the research and practice of Public International Law and International Trade Policy, Dr Owie also serves as the Chairman & CEO of Numelec Africa Holdings (NAH), the strategic partner of Numelec SA Group in the Continent of Africa.

Numelec SA of Geneva is a global leader in providing a uniquely broad spectrum of innovative medical diagnostic and healthcare infrastructure solutions in today's rapidly changing healthcare environment. Over the last Seventy (70) Years, Numelec has remained at the top in the development & marketing of cutting-edge healthcare products & solutions, with an operations network spanning over 90 countries across 5 Continents. NAH warehouses the African operations of the Numelec SA Group, coordinating a continent-wide procurement and project management network covering over thirty (50) African countries. In his role as Chairman & CEO of NAH, Dr Owie coordinates the conceptualization and implementation of all Numelec's healthcare infrastructure and renewable energy projects across Africa.
